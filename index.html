About

I am a Postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Thermomechanics of the Czech Academy of Sciences in Prague, Czech Republic. I run numerical simulations of flow in the atmosphere. I also try to predict pullutants in air using machine learning models.

I am also a teacher of the Flow in Atmospheric Boundary Layer course at the Faculty of Environmental Sciences, Czech University of Life Sciences in Prague.

I am interested in data analysis and visualization , numerical modeling and machine learning models.

I enjoy working with Python, R , HTML, LaTeX and OpenFOAM.

Skills

I like programming in Python and R for analysing and visualization of data. I also know how to work in Matlab, C++, HTML, CSS and a little bit of JavaScript. I love writing documents and presentations in LaTeX, but I also know how to use MS Office. For numerical modelling of flow in the atmosphere, I use OpenFOAM with FreeCAD for creating geometry and ParaView for vizualization of results.
